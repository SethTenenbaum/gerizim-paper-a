\title{Selected-Frame Longitudinal Organization in UNESCO Cultural
Heritage Sites:\\
Within-Representation Diagnostics for a 3\textdegree{} Harmonic Frame}

\author{Seth Tenenbaum\thanks{ORCID: \href{https://orcid.org/0009-0008-5797-2498}{0009-0008-5797-2498}.
Correspondence: sethtenenbaum@protonmail.com}\\
Independent Scholar, USA}

\maketitle

\abstract{
The full-corpus Rayleigh test for 3\textdegree{} spectral periodicity
in UNESCO Cultural and Mixed World Heritage longitudes
($N = \NclusterTotal$) is null ($p = \fullRayleighPermP$), and a global
anchor-discovery sweep is marginal ($p = \anchorMaxPermP$); against
these boundary conditions, this paper reports a selected-frame count
excess under a fixed 3\textdegree{} harmonic-deviation representation
referenced to a Levantine $\sim$35\textdegree{}E corridor, for which
Mount Gerizim (\GerizimLon\textdegree{}E) is one historically motivated
candidate point rather than a uniquely identified anchor. The target
claim is representational, not invariant: the chosen frame yields one
primary Tier-A+ excess in the UNESCO corpus, characterized through
several diagnostic angles, with a related 3\textdegree{} pattern in two
Eurasian corpora that share the corpus's geography.

\textit{Within-frame characterization.} Inside the fixed frame, the
full corpus shows Tier-A+ harmonic-node enrichment
($\clusterEnrichAp\times$, $p_\mathrm{adj} =
\BonfAllSurviveclusterApBinom$). This count excess is further
characterized by: (i) a Spearman-style cluster-asymmetry pattern in
which harmonic bands containing a precisely aligned site host
$\clusterHarmonicRatio\times$ more sites than empty bands
($p_\mathrm{adj} = \BonfAllSurviveclusterHarmonicMWp$); (ii) a
phase-rotation null in which the selected phase ranks at the
\phaseFullGerPctile\textsuperscript{th} percentile of all
\phaseNphases{} envelope phases; (iii) Tier-A++ enrichment in the
domed/spherical sub-population ($\circEnrichApp\times$,
$p_\mathrm{adj} = \BonfAllSurvivepCircApp$), with leave-3-out
worst-case robustness but with Null~A consistent with the pattern
being a consequence of dome-site geography; (iv) a unit sweep in which
3\textdegree{} is the dominant spacing and adjacent non-harmonic units
(1.8\textdegree{}, 2.1\textdegree{}, 2.7\textdegree{},
3.3\textdegree{}, 3.6\textdegree{}) are null, while 4\textdegree{} and
8\textdegree{} reduce to nodes shared with the 3\textdegree{} grid;
and (v) the same 3\textdegree{} frame producing related structure in
the Wikidata Q180987 stupa corpus and the OWTRAD attested Silk Road
network. Across the full audit ($k = \NtotalTests$),
\NsurviveBonfAll{} results survive Bonferroni correction.

\textit{Scope.} The dome A++ result is sharper than its longitude
footprint under a restricted-draw null (Null~C, marginal across three
windows) but is consistent with within-dome geographic concentration
under Null~A; it is therefore reported as a sub-population
characterization within the selected frame, not as independent evidence
of coordinated placement. The Wikidata and OWTRAD analyses are not
geography-independent replications: UNESCO cultural sites, Wikidata
stupas, and OWTRAD route nodes are all concentrated in the Eurasian
corridor. Their shared 3\textdegree{} agreement is nevertheless the
most interesting empirical feature of the analysis and warrants
further investigation. The priority next step is application of the
frozen pipeline by an independent team to heritage corpora outside the
Eurasian corridor. Pre-modern sub-degree continental longitude
determination is not a documented capability; we propose no historical
mechanism, and we read the selected-frame organization as a
hypothesis-generating regularity whose historical status is open. The
analytical choices were made in contact with the data, and the
pipeline, data, and reproduction scripts are openly deposited (Zenodo
\texttt{10.5281/zenodo.19938283}).
}
