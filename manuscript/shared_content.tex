\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:introduction}

This study evaluates a pre-specified 3\textdegree{} longitudinal lattice
anchored to Mount Gerizim and tests whether the UNESCO Cultural and Mixed
World Heritage corpus ($N = \NclusterTotal$) exhibits non-random alignment
with that grid.
The analysis does not identify the origin of the lattice; it tests whether
the observed spatial distribution is statistically consistent with this
specified structure.

The analysis separates two distinct inferential stages.
\textbf{Stage~1} (spacing): a Monte Carlo unit sweep tests for non-random
periodic structure across candidate spacings and identifies 3\textdegree{}
as the interval that produces the most concentrated deviations
(\S\ref{sec:results_unit_sweep}).
\textbf{Stage~2} (phase): conditional on that spacing, specific
historically motivated reference phases are evaluated against permutation
and parametric-bootstrap nulls (\S\ref{sec:statistical_framework}).
Because phase is not uniquely identifiable from the data alone,
Stage~2 does not constitute discovery of an origin meridian but rather
a test of consistency with proposed reference frames
(\S\ref{sec:anchor}).

Three primary threshold-free tests from Stage~2 survive Holm correction
at $k = \BonfK$.
A phase-rotation null tests whether the Gerizim phase minimises the
full-corpus mean harmonic deviation against a uniform random phase offset
($p_\mathrm{adj} = \pHolmTestThree$, Test~3).
A cluster asymmetry test finds that harmonic nodes near the Gerizim phase
carry disproportionately large site clusters (Spearman $\rho$, permutation null;
$p_\mathrm{adj} = \pHolmTestTwo$, Test~2).
A dome von~Mises mixture confirms the concentration in the dome sub-corpus
($p_\mathrm{adj} = \pHolmTestOne$, Test~1).
A phase-envelope scan confirms the signal is concentrated at the Gerizim phase
(\S\ref{sec:results_phase_localization}).
A global anchor sweep confirms stability across the $\sim$35\textdegree{}E
corridor (\S\ref{sec:sensitivity_global}).

Phase estimates vary under discretization (e.g., tier thresholds), as expected.
However, the threshold-free tests and anchor sweeps consistently identify a broad
phase corridor near $\sim$35\textdegree{}E, indicating that the signal is robust
at the level of a band rather than a uniquely determined point
(\S\ref{sec:anchor}).

A cross-corpus enrichment analysis finds that the UNESCO dome subset, the full
UNESCO cultural corpus, the external Wikidata Q180987 stupa inventory, and the
OWTRAD Silk Road route network each show peak enrichment within approximately
\multiscaleJointMaxKm{}~km of a harmonic longitude line --- one-tenth of the
$\approx$289~km inter-node spacing at 30\textdegree{}N
($p = \multiscaleJointMaxP$, \multiscaleJointMaxPSig{};
\multiscaleNPerm{} permutations; \S\ref{sec:multiscale}).
The three nested proximity windows (Tiers A++, A+, A; \S\ref{sec:tier_classification})
span this same range and are reported as supporting descriptive context
(\S\ref{sec:results_global}--\ref{sec:resultsevolution_inline}).

\section{Background}
\label{sec:prior}
%------------------------------------------------------------------------------



\subsection{Prior Work on Spatial Periodicity and Sacred Landscapes}

Statistical evaluation of spatial periodicity in monument distributions has a
long and contested history.
Thom's ``megalithic yard'' \citep{thom1967,thom1971,thom1964}
stimulated statistical refinement \citep{kendall1974} and methodological
critique \citep{freeman1976}.
Magli \citep{magli2013,magli2016} has applied Monte Carlo methods to
archaeoastronomical alignments, and Ruggles and Silva have advanced
probability-based frameworks for evaluating orientation claims
\citep{ruggles1999astronomy,ruggles2015handbook,silva2020statistical}.
The ICOMOS-IAU thematic study \citep{ruggles2010icomos} provides
institutional context for archaeoastronomical analysis of World Heritage
properties.

\subsection{Reference Phase and Candidate Anchors}
\label{sec:anchor}

The harmonic deviations in this study are computed from Mount Gerizim
(\GerizimLon\textdegree{}E) as the \textit{a priori} reference phase.
Gerizim is chosen because of its independently documented
\textit{tabbur ha-aretz} (``navel of the earth'') cosmographic role in
Samaritan tradition \citep{purvis1968,macdonald1964,crown1989}, a
justification that predates the analysis entirely.

\textbf{Phase robustness and the limit of anchor identification.}
Phase estimates vary under discretization (e.g., tier thresholds), as expected.
However, threshold-free methods and anchor sweeps consistently identify a broad
phase corridor near $\sim$35\textdegree{}E, indicating that the signal is robust
at the level of a band rather than a uniquely determined point.
The primary finding of this paper is therefore the existence and phase-specificity
of the periodicity itself; Mount Gerizim is presented as the historically motivated
candidate within that corridor, not as a conclusion the statistical analysis can
establish independently.

A global anchor sweep (\S\ref{sec:sensitivity_global}) confirms the result is
not sensitive to the exact coordinate within the corridor: both Gerizim and
Jerusalem (\JerusalemLon\textdegree{}E, $\approx$\GerizimJerusalemKm~km south)
place the \xbandLabel\textdegree{}E band in the global top
\anchorSweepTopPct\% of \anchorSweepNanchors{} trial anchors.
Mecca ($p \approx \pMeccaAnchor$, \pMeccaAnchorSig{}),
Mt.\ Meru ($p \approx \pMeruAnchor$, \pMeruAnchorSig{}), and
Mt.\ Kailash ($p \approx \pKailashAnchor$, \pKailashAnchorSig{}) yield
no significant enrichment, confirming the periodicity is phase-specific
to the $\sim$35\textdegree{}E corridor.

Gerizim's coordinate is from the UNESCO tentative list (ref.~5706, DMS
\GerizimLonDMS{}, State of Palestine, 2012;
\url{https://whc.unesco.org/en/tentativelists/5706/}) and is not inscribed.
Jerusalem is an inscribed corpus member, self-excluded when used as anchor.

\section{Data and Methods}
\label{sec:methods}
%------------------------------------------------------------------------------

\subsection{Corpus Construction}
\label{sec:corpus}

The UNESCO World Heritage XML dataset \citep{unescowhc2024xml} contains
$\NwhcTotal$ inscribed nominations. UNESCO designates each site
\textit{Cultural} (criteria i--vi), \textit{Natural} (criteria vii--x),
or \textit{Mixed}; sites with cultural heritage alongside natural values
receive Mixed and are included here.
Retaining Cultural and Mixed ($-\NexcNatural$ Natural-only) and
excluding $\NexcNoCoord$ entries lacking geocoordinates (both linear
route networks) yields $N = \NclusterTotal$ sites.

\subsection{Geodetic Framework}
\label{sec:geodetic}

The reference anchor is Mount Gerizim at \GerizimLon\textdegree{}E
(see \S\ref{sec:anchor}). Gerizim is not inscribed and does not appear
in the standard corpus. Jerusalem is inscribed and scored as a regular
site in Tests~1--4 (A+ at 4.1~km under Gerizim).
For sensitivity sweeps (\S\ref{sec:sensitivity_global}), Gerizim is added
as a synthetic entry so each anchor self-excludes symmetrically.

For a site at longitude $\lambda$, the deviation from the nearest 3\textdegree{} harmonic is
$\delta = |\lambda - \GerizimLon| \bmod 3^\circ$ (folded to $[0, 1.5^\circ]$).
The tier classification is a fixed angular criterion.
Kilometer labels (e.g.\ $\lesssim\ApThreshKm{}$~km for Tier-A+) are equatorial
approximations ($\delta \times 111$~km/\textdegree{}); no latitude correction is applied.

The geometric null for Tier-A+ is $2 \times \ApThreshDeg{}^\circ / 1.5^\circ = \NullRateApPct{}\%$
(assuming uniform longitudes); a Gaussian KDE confirms this is not materially
inflated by longitude clumping ($\simKDEMean \pm \simKDEStd$ expected A+).
The primary tests use simulation-based nulls (\S\ref{sec:simulation_null}).


\subsection{Statistical Framework}
\label{sec:statistical_framework}

The 3\textdegree{}/Gerizim grid is a pre-specified hypothesis
(\S\ref{sec:anchor}); this study tests whether UNESCO sites concentrate
at its harmonics and whether that concentration is specific to this grid.
Three primary tests address this without requiring any tier cutoff or bin
boundary (full descriptions in \S\ref{sec:test_populations}):

\begin{description}
  \item[\textbf{Test~1}] Two-component von~Mises/uniform mixture fit to the
    dome sub-corpus ($N = \NcircTotal$), mean fixed at Gerizim phase, assessed
    by parametric bootstrap.
  \item[\textbf{Test~2}] Spearman rank correlation between harmonic node mean
    deviation and site count, full corpus ($N = \NclusterTotal$), permutation null.
  \item[\textbf{Test~3}] Phase-rotation null: the reference grid is shifted by a
    uniform random offset in $[0^\circ,3^\circ)$; tests whether the Gerizim phase
    minimizes the full-corpus mean harmonic deviation.
\end{description}

All three are assessed by permutation or parametric bootstrap and survive
Holm step-down correction at $k = \BonfK$ (Table~\ref{tab:bonferroni_both}).
Tier labels (A++, A+, A) defined in \S\ref{sec:tier_classification} are used
as descriptive vocabulary only; they do not enter any primary test statistic.

\subsection{Tier Classification}
\label{sec:tier_classification}

Sites are assigned to tiers by their angular deviation $\delta$ from the
nearest 3\textdegree{} harmonic node.
The three nested A-windows span the same range of proximities examined
in the multi-scale enrichment analysis (\S\ref{sec:multiscale}):
4\% null ($\approx$\AppThreshKm{}~km) through 20\% null ($\approx$\ATierThreshKm{}~km),
covering the full interval over which the four corpora show enrichment.
These windows are not identified as peaks; they are fixed proximity bands
used as descriptive vocabulary for summarising harmonic proximity.
No primary test statistic depends on a tier cutoff.

\begin{description}
  \item[\textbf{Tier~A++}]
    $\delta \leq \AppThreshDeg{}^\circ$ ($\lesssim\AppThreshKm{}$~km); $P_0 = \NullRateApp{}$.
  \item[\textbf{Tier~A+}]
    $\delta \leq \ApThreshDeg{}^\circ$ ($\lesssim\ApThreshKm{}$~km); $P_0 = \NullRateAp{}$.
  \item[\textbf{Tier~A}]
    $\delta \leq \ATierThreshDeg{}^\circ$ ($\lesssim\ATierThreshKm{}$~km); $P_0 = \NullRateA{}$.
  \item[\textbf{Tier~B}] Middle zone, between harmonics and inter-harmonic midpoints.
  \item[\textbf{Tier~C/C$-$/C$--$}] Symmetric mirror of A/A+/A++, measured from the
    nearest inter-harmonic midpoint.
\end{description}


\subsection{Test Populations}
\label{sec:test_populations}

\subsubsection*{Primary threshold-free tests (Tests~1--3)}

Tests~1--3 are described in full in \S\ref{sec:statistical_framework}.

The only additional detail specific to this section is the keyword
population for Test~1: \NcircKeywords{} morphological keywords from four
roots (\textit{stupa/stupas}, \textit{tholos}, \textit{dome/domed/domes},
\textit{spherical}) are matched as whole words against each site's name,
XML description, and extended OUV text ($N = \NcircTotal$).
The context-validated subset ($N = \NcircValidated$) restricts to sites
where the keyword appears in an architectural context and is reported as
a robustness check (Test~5x).

\subsubsection*{Supporting enrichment tests (Tests~4--6)}
\label{sec:enrichment_tests}

Tests~4--6 count sites falling within a fixed proximity window of the
nearest harmonic node and test whether the rate exceeds a geometric null.
Three nested windows are reported (Tiers~A++, A+, A; null rates \NullRateApp{}, \NullRateAp{}, \NullRateA{};
widths \AppThreshDeg{}$^\circ$, \ApThreshDeg{}$^\circ$, \ATierThreshDeg{}$^\circ$ on $[0^\circ, 1.5^\circ)$).
No primary test depends on a tier cutoff (see \S\ref{sec:tier_classification}).


\textbf{Test~4 (full-corpus enrichment).}
Full corpus ($N = \NclusterTotal$), no keyword filter.
Binomial test of A+ rate vs.\ $p_0 = \NullRateAp{}$ null.

\textbf{Test~5 (dome enrichment).}
Dome keyword subset ($N = \NcircTotal$).
Fisher exact and binomial tests of A+ and A++ rates.

\textbf{Test~6 (mound evolution, sensitivity).}
Extends the dome subset with \textit{tumulus, tumuli, barrow, barrows, kofun}
($N = \NevoTotal$); not in the primary Bonferroni family.

\subsubsection*{Descriptive and post-hoc tests}

\textbf{Test~7 (temporal gradient).}
Cochran-Armitage trend test on inscription year. Descriptive only.

\textbf{Test~8 (founding classifier, post-hoc).}
Keyword classifier; no weight in the primary evidential chain
(Appendix~\ref{sec:kwdaudit_founding}).

\subsection{Multiple-Comparisons Protocol}
\label{sec:bonferroni}

\textbf{Primary family ($k = \BonfK$, Holm step-down).}
Tests~1--3 are all threshold-free; they form the primary Bonferroni family.

\textbf{Supporting enrichment tests (Tests~4--5).}
Tests~4 and~5 use a pre-specified proximity window; not in the primary family.
Results are in the FDR audit (Table~\ref{tab:bonferroni_both}; \S\ref{sec:fdraudit}).

\textbf{Other.}
Test~6 is a sensitivity variant of Test~5.
Test~7 (temporal gradient) is descriptive.
Test~8 is post-hoc.
Religion sub-group analyses (\S\ref{sec:resultsrelig}) and diagnostic
Tests~A--D (\S\ref{sec:results_rayleigh}) are not Bonferroni-corrected.

\label{sec:methods_simulation}

Three simulation approaches (
\simNperms{} trials each) and two dedicated geographic-concentration nulls

\section{Results}

\label{sec:results}

The analysis proceeds in three evidential steps: (i)~three threshold-free
primary tests (von~Mises mixture, Spearman cluster asymmetry, phase-rotation
null) that do not require any bin boundary
(\S\ref{sec:resultscluster}, \S\ref{sec:resultscircular}); (ii)~phase localization
showing the signal is concentrated at the Gerizim-grid phase rather than
uniformly distributed (\S\ref{sec:results_phase_localization}); and
(iii)~a spacing-specificity check confirming the result is not reproduced
by nearby non-harmonic intervals (\S\ref{sec:results_unit_sweep}).
Tier-based enrichment counts are reported as supporting descriptive context
(\S\ref{sec:results_global}--\ref{sec:resultsevolution_inline}).

%------------------------------------------------------------------------------
\subsection{Spacing Specificity: Is 3\textdegree{} Discriminated from Nearby Intervals?}
\label{sec:results_unit_sweep}
%------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The 3\textdegree{} grid spacing is motivated by prior metrological and
geographical work (\S\ref{sec:anchor}); it is a pre-specified hypothesis,
not a data-derived discovery.
The analysis here asks a different question: \emph{is the observed enrichment
specific to 3\textdegree{}, or would any nearby spacing produce the same result?}

A geographic bootstrap of the full cultural corpus (\permSlopeSpecN{} trials,
longitude-preserving permutation) finds no random spacing in the canonical range
reproduces the observed tier-bin peak at either A$^{+}$ or A$^{++}$
($p = \permSlopeCanonBestJointFullAP{}$, $p = \permSlopeCanonBestJointFullAPP{}$,
both \permSlopeCanonBestJointSigFullAP{}).
Restricting to morphologically unambiguous spherical forms (stupa, tholos,
$n = \permSlopeKwN{}$) is significant at the A tier
($p = \permSlopeCanonBestJointKwA{}$, \permSlopeCanonBestJointSigKwA{}).
A $\pm 0.3\%$ shift from exactly 3\textdegree{} collapses this peak in at
least one population, confirming the result is not robust to small
perturbations of the assumed spacing (\S\ref{sec:metrology}).

The 1.5\textdegree{} and 6\textdegree{} spacings produce similar tier-bin
counts, but both are mathematical consequences of the 3\textdegree{}
structure (every 3\textdegree{} harmonic is also a 1.5\textdegree{} harmonic;
6\textdegree{} harmonics are a strict subset), so neither constitutes an
independent signal.
A degree-denominated Monte Carlo sweep (\S\ref{sec:limitations_geo}) finds
candidate intervals at 4\textdegree{} and 8\textdegree{} also reach nominal
dome significance, but 6 of 7 and all 4 of their hits respectively fall on
nodes shared with the 3\textdegree{} grid, confirming they are arithmetic
consequences rather than independent angular units.

In summary, the enrichment is \emph{specific} to the pre-specified 3\textdegree{}
spacing: no nearby non-harmonic spacing replicates the signal, and small
perturbations destroy it.

%------------------------------------------------------------------------------
\subsection{Phase localization}
\label{sec:results_phase_localization}
%------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Having identified 3\textdegree{} as the dominant period, we ask whether the
signal is uniformly distributed over all possible phases or concentrated at a
specific phase.
A phase-envelope scan over all origins $\phi \in [0,3^\circ)$
(\phaseNphases{} phases at \phaseStep\textdegree{} resolution) shows a strong
preferred phase: full-corpus A+ counts span
\phaseFullMin{}--\phaseFullMax{} (median \phaseFullMedian{}), and Gerizim's phase
($\phaseGerizimPhase^\circ$ mod 3\textdegree{}) sits at the
\phaseFullGerPctile\textsuperscript{th} percentile (\phaseFullGerCount{} A+),
the \phaseDomeGerPctile\textsuperscript{th} for domes
(\phaseDomeGerCount/\NcircTotal{}; range \phaseDomeMin{}--\phaseDomeMax{}).
The Rayleigh permutation test on the 3\textdegree{}-circle
yields $p_\mathrm{perm} = \rayleighPermP$~\rayleighPermPSig{}
(\S\ref{sec:phase_peak_formal}).
The signal is therefore phase-localized, not phase-uniform; subsequent
tests characterize it relative to Gerizim, the historically attested phase
reference (\S\ref{sec:anchor}).

\subsection{Cluster Asymmetry (Tests~2--3, Primary)}
\label{sec:resultscluster}

Full corpus ($N = \NclusterTotal$), no keyword filter (see \S\ref{sec:test_populations}).
Two threshold-free primary tests (Table~\ref{tab:bonferroni_both}) quantify the
structural signature of 3\textdegree{} periodicity without any tier cutoff.
\textbf{Test~2} (Spearman rank correlation between harmonic node mean deviation and site
count): $\rho = \spearmanDevClusterRho$, permutation $p = \spearmanDevClusterP$
(\spearmanDevClusterPSig{}); nodes drawing more sites show smaller mean harmonic
deviations.
\textbf{Test~3} (phase-rotation null): the observed full-corpus mean harmonic deviation
is smaller than expected when the reference grid is rotated by a uniform random offset
in $[0^\circ, 3^\circ)$ ($p_\mathrm{perm} = \fullMeanDevPermP$, \fullMeanDevPermPSig{}).
Both survive Holm correction at $k = \BonfK$ ($p_\mathrm{adj} = \pHolmTestTwo$~\pHolmTestTwoSig{};
$p_\mathrm{adj} = \pHolmTestThree$~\pHolmTestThreeSig{}).

\paragraph*{Subtest: does clustering produce the unit-sensitivity peak?}
\label{sec:cluster_cond_sensitivity}
A joint permutation test (\clusterCondN{} iterations) finds the conditional
fraction of clustering-matched permutations producing the canonical-unit peak
(\clusterCondPeakFrac{}) is identical to the unconditional rate
(\clusterCondPeakFracUncond{}): the clustering and unit-sensitivity signals
are statistically independent.

\paragraph*{Methodological note: Rayleigh phase-lock (Test~B).}
\label{sec:results_rayleigh}
The near-perfect Rayleigh $R$ of the A+ sub-sample is a mathematical
consequence of the A+ classification criterion (\S\ref{sec:circularity})
and is reported as a structural description.
Three formal permutation tests (\NpermRayleigh{} shuffles each, seed~\simSeed{})
address distinct aspects of the
periodicity:

\textbf{Test~A (full-corpus Rayleigh, negative control).}
Full-corpus Rayleigh $R = \fullRayleighR$
($Z = \fullRayleighZ$, $p_\mathrm{perm} = \fullRayleighPermP$, \fullRayleighPermPSig{}):
no spurious global 3\textdegree{} periodicity; signal is concentrated in A+ sites.

\textbf{Test~B (A+-only Rayleigh, structural check).}
Among \Nap{} Tier-A+ sites, $R = \rayleighR$ ($Z = \rayleighZ$):
a structural consequence of the A+ criterion, not independent evidence
(\S\ref{sec:circularity}).

\textbf{Tests~C--E (anchor specificity).}
Circular-shift null: \anchorShiftApCount{} A+ sites vs.\ null mean \anchorShiftNullMu{} ($p_\mathrm{perm} = \anchorShiftPermP$, \anchorShiftPermPSig{}).
Targeted von~Mises (full corpus, $\mu_0$ fixed at Gerizim phase): $\hat{w} = \targetedWfull$ (${\approx}\targetedWfullPct\%$ of sites), $p_\mathrm{boot} = \targetedPfull$ (\targetedPfullSig{}).
Global anchor sweep ($\anchorSweepNanchors{}$ trial anchors): Levantine corridor $p = \anchorMaxPermP$ (\anchorMaxPermPSig{}).

\subsection{Full-Corpus Enrichment (Test~4, Supporting)}
\label{sec:results_global}

Having established that the 3\textdegree{} period is real and phase-localized,
we now quantify the excess at the Gerizim-grid phase.

\begin{figure*}[!t]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.9\textwidth]{../figures/fig_devhist}
\caption{Distribution of harmonic deviations for the full UNESCO corpus.
  The excess of sites within the Tier-A+ window ($\delta \leq \ApThreshDeg{}^\circ$,
  shaded) above the uniform null (dashed line) establishes the corpus-level
  signal against which Tests~1 and~2 (domed monuments and cluster asymmetry)
  demonstrate morphological and spatial specificity.}
\label{fig:devhist}

The full UNESCO Cultural/Mixed corpus ($N = \NclusterTotal$) shows a
Tier-A+ rate of \clusterApRate\% (Figure~\ref{fig:devhist})
(\NclusterAp/\NclusterTotal; Clopper-Pearson 95\% CI
[\clusterApCIlo\%, \clusterApCIhi\%]),
above the \NullRateAp{} null
(binomial $p = \clusterApBinom$; Bonferroni $p_{\mathrm{adj}} = \pAdjTestOne$,
$k = \BonfK$).
The observed enrichment is $\clusterEnrichAp\times$, modest relative
to the dome subpopulation ($\circEnrichAp\times$) and the harmonic cluster
asymmetry. For the primary family (Tests~1--2, $k=\BonfK$), 
both pass the Bonferroni bound at $k = \BonfK$
(dome $p_\mathrm{adj} = \pAdjTestTwo$~\pAdjTestTwoSig{},
cluster $p_\mathrm{adj} = \pAdjTestThree$~\pAdjTestThreeSig{}).

\textbf{Region-stratified robustness.}
A region-specific empirical null (Fisher combination of per-region binomials)
yields $p = \pRegionEmpAll$ (\pRegionEmpAllSig{}); the pre-2000 cohort remains significant
against the geometric null ($p = \pPreTwoK$~\pPreTwoKSig{}; \S\ref{sec:resultstemporal}).
Both results are included in the FDR audit (Table~\ref{tab:bonferroni_both}).

\subsubsection*{World religion sites}
\label{sec:resultsrelig}
Religion sub-populations are defined by keyword sets documented in the supplementary audit archive~\citep{tenenbaum2026audit}.
A chi-squared test finds no significant A+ rate difference across the five main
religion groups ($\chi^2 = \religChiSqStat$, df\,=\,\religChiSqDof,
$p = \pReligChiSq$, \pReligChiSqSig{}).
Both Buddhism (\NbudATier/\NbudSitesRelig{} A-tier, \budATierRate\%;
\NbudInterHarmonic/\NbudSitesRelig{} C-band, \budInterHarmonicRate\%;
$p = \pBudJoint$~\pBudJointSig{}) and Hinduism (\NhinATier/\NhinSites{} A-tier, \hinATierRate\%;
\NhinInterHarmonic/\NhinSites{} C-band, \hinInterHarmonicRate\%;
$p = \pHinJoint$~\pHinJointSig{}) show a joint A-tier/C-band signal
(multivariate hypergeometric); both persist in tradition-exclusive sites
($p = \pBudExclJoint$~\pBudExclJointSig{}, $p = \pHinExclJoint$~\pHinExclJointSig{}).

\subsection{Domed and Spherical Monuments (Test~5, Supporting)}
\label{sec:resultscircular}

The keyword filter is described in \S\ref{sec:test_populations} ($N = \NcircTotal$).
\textbf{Test~1} (dome von~Mises mixture, Table~\ref{tab:bonferroni_both}): a two-component
von~Mises/uniform mixture with the mean fixed at the Gerizim phase yields
$\hat{w} = \targetedWdome$ ($\hat{\kappa} = \targetedKdome$),
$p_\mathrm{boot} = \targetedPdome$ (\targetedPdomeSig{};
Holm $p_\mathrm{adj} = \pHolmTestOne$, \pHolmTestOneSig{}).
Approximately $\targetedWdomePct\%$ of dome sites are concentrated at the Gerizim phase,
with an angular spread of ${\approx}\targetedSdDomeDeg$\textdegree{}.
The tier-based enrichment below provides complementary descriptive context.

\textbf{Tier-A++ concentration (key result).}
\NcircTierApp{} of \NcircTotal{} dome sites (\NcircAppRate\%, $\circEnrichApp\times$ the \NullRateApp{} null)
fall within Tier-A++ ($\delta \leq \AppThreshDeg{}^\circ$, $\lesssim\AppThreshKm{}$~km).
This concentration is significant and reproducible across three test statistics:
Fisher OR~$= \circAppFisherOR$, $p = \pCircAppFisher$~\pCircAppFisherSig{};
permutation $Z = \circAppPermZ$, $p = \circAppPermP$~\pCircAppPermSig{};
bootstrap $p = \circAppBootP$~\pCircAppBootSig{}.
Dome A++ survives full Bonferroni correction at $k = \NtotalTests$ (\S\ref{sec:multiple_comparisons}).

\subsection{Unit Sweep and Sensitivity Slope}
\label{sec:metrology}

The unit sweep and sensitivity slope results are reported in

\subsection{Global Anchor Sweep}
\label{sec:sensitivity_global}

A global sweep (0--360\textdegree{}E, 0.01\textdegree{} resolution,
\anchorSweepNanchors{} trial anchors) assesses whether the
$\sim$35\textdegree{}E corridor is unusual globally.
The sweep uses the extended corpus described in \S\ref{sec:geodetic}
($N_\mathrm{ext} = 1012$), each anchor excluding its own entry,
so the focal anchors test against $N = \NgerizimSweepCorpus$.

Both anchors place the corridor in the global top \anchorSweepTopPct\%
(\anchorSweepNanchors{} trial anchors, 0--360\textdegree{}E;
Gerizim: \GerizimSweepAp{} A+, \anchorSweepPctile\textsuperscript{th} percentile;

\subsubsection*{The $\xbandLabel$\textdegree{}E periodic structure}
\label{sec:phase_peak_artifact}

Every $\xbandLabel$\textdegree{}E longitude (\NharmonicsCoarse{} anchors)
produces identically the maximum A+ count, spaced 3\textdegree{} apart.
The flat-top width is a function of the tier threshold chosen:
A++ ($\pm\AppThreshDeg{}^\circ$), A+ ($\pm\ApThreshDeg{}^\circ$), A ($\pm\ATierThreshDeg{}^\circ$);
the number of tied anchors is therefore a methodological artifact, not a physical feature.
Mount Gerizim is a fixed landmark at \GerizimLon\textdegree{}E (phase $\GerizimPhase$\textdegree{},
gap $\phaseGap$\textdegree{} $\approx \phaseGapKm$~km); it lies within the A and A+ flat-top
bands but not A++.

\subsubsection*{Formal statistical tests for the $\xbandLabel$\textdegree{} periodicity}
\label{sec:phase_peak_formal}

The full-corpus Rayleigh ($p_\mathrm{perm} = \fullRayleighPermP$, \fullRayleighPermPSig{}) confirms no
spurious global periodicity; Test~B is tautological.
Tests~C ($p_\mathrm{perm} = \anchorShiftPermP$, \anchorShiftPermPSig{}) and~D ($p_\mathrm{boot} = \targetedPfull$, \targetedPfullSig{})
are positive anchor-specific tests; Test~E ($p = \anchorMaxPermP$, \anchorMaxPermPSig{}, marginal) is the anchor discovery correction.
The unit sensitivity sweep (\S\ref{sec:metrology}) and Monte Carlo unit sweep
(\S\ref{sec:limitations_geo}) confirm 3\textdegree{} as the dominant spacing
among candidate values, \emph{given} the pre-specified Gerizim anchor;
neither sweep constitutes anchor-free or period-free discovery.

\subsection{Null Model Validation}

\section{Limitations}
\label{sec:limitations}

\subsection{Circularity: Full Canonical Treatment}
\label{sec:limitations_circularity}
\label{sec:circularity}

Because all inputs are fully public, every step is explicitly exploratory;
no confirmatory inference is claimed.
The relevant questions are whether the pattern exists and whether the
reference phase was chosen on independent grounds.

Both are answered directly. The global anchor sweep (\S\ref{sec:sensitivity_global})
confirms that the enrichment is a property of the $\sim$35\textdegree{}E
corridor: every $\xbandLabel$\textdegree{}E anchor produces the same result, and
Gerizim and Jerusalem both place the corridor in the global top
\anchorSweepTopPct\% of \anchorSweepNanchors{} trial anchors.
Gerizim is distinguished by pre-existing documentary evidence (the \textit{tabbur ha-aretz}
cosmographic role, \S\ref{sec:anchor}) predating this analysis.

The 3\textdegree{} structure is recoverable by any unanchored
\NharmonicsCoarse-phase scan. Dome enrichment (Test~2) is identical across
all \NharmonicsCoarse{} $\xbandLabel$\textdegree{}E anchors. Cluster
asymmetry (Test~3) requires no keyword filter and no anchor.
Anchor-discovery correction (Test~E, $p = \anchorMaxPermP$,
\anchorMaxPermPSig{}) is marginal; primary evidence is Tests~2 and~3.

\subsection{Geographic and Cultural Selection Bias}
\label{sec:limitations_geo}

The UNESCO list over-represents Europe and the
Mediterranean-to-South-Asian corridor of Cultural/Mixed sites. 
The regional distribution is as follows: 
(Europe \& North America: \CorpusPctEurope\%;
Arab States: \CorpusPctArabStates\%;
Asia-Pacific: \CorpusPctAsiaPac\%;
Latin Americas: \CorpusPctLatAm\%;
Africa: \CorpusPctAfrica\%).
Domed monuments exceed the A+ rate of other monuments from the same longitude
footprint (Null~C, $p = \geoNullDomeRestrictedP$, \geoNullDomeRestrictedPSig{}), establishing
that the enrichment is carried by architectural type, not location alone.

A Monte Carlo unit sweep (\mcNperms{} permutations, \mcNcandidates{} candidate
spacings 0.5\textdegree{}--15\textdegree{}) finds the dominant peak at 3\textdegree{}
($p < 0.001$), with harmonics at 6\textdegree{} and 12\textdegree{} also significant.
The dome subset yields \mcDomeNsigCollapsed{} independent dominant spacings
($P = \mcDomeCollapsedPEqOne{}$ under the geographic null).

\textbf{Periodogram and variable anchors.}
An omnidirectional Rayleigh periodogram finds the period with maximum phase
concentration across all phases; the dominant period and optimal phase both
vary by sub-population, so 3\textdegree{}/Gerizim is not the raw spectral peak
in any single corpus.
Its evidential weight rests on cross-corpus consistency: the UNESCO dome subset
(Tests~1--3), Wikidata Q180987 stupas (\S\ref{sec:stupas_q180987}), and the
OWTRAD network (\S\ref{sec:transmission}) all show enrichment at the same
pre-specified period and anchor.

\subsection{Americas Negative Control}
\label{sec:americas_negative_control}
The UNESCO Americas sub-corpus ($N = \AmericasN$) shows A+ rate
\AmericasApRate\%, below the \NullRateAp{} null
($p = \AmericasOneSidedP$~\AmericasOneSidedStar{}, one-sided depletion).

\subsection{Other Limitations}

\textbf{Geocoding precision.}
UNESCO XML coordinates are administrative centroids (unsystematic noise, no directional bias).

\textbf{Metrological evidence.}
No ancient surveying instrument calibrated to metrological sub-units has been
recovered; the b\={e}ru \citep{hunger2019mul,powell1987metrological} is noted
as historical coincidence. All thresholds are degree-denominated.

\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}

\subsection{Alternative Explanations}
\label{sec:alt_explanations}

\textbf{(1) Independent convergence.}
Hemispherical structures recur across cultures
\citep{aveni2001skywatchers,aveni2003archaeoastronomy}, but convergence
predicts no longitude specificity.

\textbf{(2) Geographic concentration.}
Null~C: other monuments from the same longitude footprint are
significantly less enriched ($p = \geoNullDomeRestrictedP$,
\geoNullDomeRestrictedPSig{}); the Monte Carlo unit sweep
(\S\ref{sec:limitations_geo}) confirms 3\textdegree{} as the dominant spacing
given the pre-specified Gerizim anchor, ruling out the explanation that the
enrichment reflects only the concentration of cultural sites in the
35\textdegree{}E longitude corridor.

\textbf{(3) Random coincidence.}
The KDE-smoothed null confirms dome enrichment ($p = \simKDEP$~\simKDEPSig{});
permutation ($p = \simDomePermP$~\simDomePermPSig{}) and bootstrap
($p = \simDomeBootP$~\simDomeBootPSig{}) are marginal at the A+ level
but highly significant for the A++ sub-population
(permutation $p = \circAppPermP$~\pCircAppPermSig{};
bootstrap $p = \circAppBootP$~\pCircAppBootSig{}).
Tests~C and~D (\S\ref{sec:results_rayleigh}) confirm anchor specificity and periodicity structure.

\subsection{Independent Validation: OWTRAD and Wikidata Corpora}
\label{sec:transmission}

The 3\textdegree{} period identified in the UNESCO corpus is independently
confirmed in two external datasets. First, the OWTRAD attested-route
network \citep{ciolek2004owtrad} (\NOwtradEdges{} route segments,
\NOwtradVertices{} unique endpoints).
Testing all \NOwtradDegW{} endpoint positions across the full edge list
(each city weighted by the number of segments it connects),
\NOwtradDegWApp{} fall at A++ harmonics, \NOwtradDegWAp{} at A+, and \NOwtradDegWA{} at A
($\owtradDegWAppEnrich\times$ null rate, $z = \zOwtradDegWApp$,
$p = \pOwtradDegWApp$~\pOwtradDegWAppSig{} for A++;
$p = \pOwtradDegWAp$~\pOwtradDegWApSig{} for A+;
$p = \pOwtradDegWA$~\pOwtradDegWASig{} for A; one-tailed binomial).
Testing each unique endpoint once gives
\NOwtradVertexApp{}/\NOwtradVertices{} at A++, \NOwtradVertexAp{} at A+, and \NOwtradVertexA{} at A
($\owtradVertexAppEnrich\times$, $z = \zOwtradVertexApp$,
$p = \pOwtradVertexApp$~\pOwtradVertexAppSig{} for A++;
$p = \pOwtradVertexAp$~\pOwtradVertexApSig{} for A+;
$p = \pOwtradVertexA$~\pOwtradVertexASig{} for A; one-tailed binomial).
A phase-shift test (random offset of the 3\textdegree{} grid,
\NOwtradPerms{} iterations) gives $p = \pOwtradVertexAppPhase$~\pOwtradVertexAppPhaseSig{},
confirming the enrichment is specific to the Gerizim anchor.
A cluster asymmetry test mirrors Test~3 of the primary family:
harmonics containing at least one A++ endpoint (\NOwtradAppHarmonics{} harmonics)
carry \owtradClustDegRatio$\times$ more total route-connectivity than
harmonics without one (\NOwtradNonAppHarmonics{} harmonics;
$p = \pOwtradClustDeg$~\pOwtradClustDegSig{}, Mann-Whitney one-tailed).
The Silk Road corridor is illustrated in Figure~\ref{fig:geo_trail}; harmonic tier distributions for both OWTRAD populations are shown in Figure~\ref{fig:owtrad_tiers}.

\section{Conclusion}

The UNESCO Cultural and Mixed corpus shows significant 3\textdegree{} longitudinal
enrichment with all three primary tests surviving Bonferroni correction at $k=\BonfK$.
Longitude bands containing a precisely aligned site are $\clusterHarmonicRatio\times$ more
densely populated than bands without one.
A multi-scale combined test across \multiscaleK{} log-spaced proximity windows
confirms the result is not an artifact of any fixed tier choice
(dome subset $p = \multiscaleDomeCombinedP$~\multiscaleDomeCombinedPSig{});
joint enrichment across four independent corpora peaks at approximately
\multiscaleJointMaxKm{} km from a harmonic node
($p = \multiscaleJointMaxP$~\multiscaleJointMaxPSig{}).
The Wikidata Q180987 stupa corpus and the OWTRAD route network independently
confirm related aspects of the same periodic pattern.

\section{Von Mises Mixture Characterisation of External Corpora}
\label{app:vonmises}

A von~Mises + uniform mixture model
$f(\theta|\kappa) = w \cdot \mathrm{VM}(0,\kappa) + (1-w)\cdot\mathrm{Uniform}(0,2\pi)$
was fitted to the harmonic phase deviations ($\theta \in [0,\pi]$, $\theta=0$
on-harmonic) of two external corpora using maximum likelihood with a
grid of starting values.

OWTRAD Silk Road nodes ($N = \vmNOwtrad{}$):
$\hat{\kappa} = \vmKappaOwtrad{}$, $\hat{w} = \vmMixtureWeightOwtrad{}$,
$\hat{\sigma} = \kappa^{-1/2} = \vmSigmaDegOwtrad{}^\circ$.

Wikidata Q180987 stupas ($N = \vmNStupa{}$):
$\hat{\kappa} = \vmKappaStupa{}$, $\hat{w} = \vmMixtureWeightStupa{}$,
$\hat{\sigma} = \vmSigmaDegStupa{}^\circ$.

$N$-weighted pooled $\hat{\sigma} = \vmSigmaDegPooled{}^\circ$;
$\kappa$ ratio = \vmKappaRatio{} across corpora.

These numbers characterize the angular clustering of the external corpora.
They are not used to derive the UNESCO tier boundaries or any inferential
threshold in this paper. The $\hat{\sigma}$ multiples
($0.25\hat{\sigma} = \vmHalfSigmaDegStupa{}^\circ$,
$0.50\hat{\sigma} = \vmOneSigmaDegStupa{}^\circ$,
$1.00\hat{\sigma} = \vmTwoSigmaDegStupa{}^\circ$) agree with the
tier widths to within rounding (Table~\ref{tab:tiers}), which is noted
as descriptive context only.

\section{Tier-Threshold Sensitivity Audit}
\label{app:tier_sensitivity}

The primary enrichment tests use Tier-A+ ($P_0 = 4\%$, $\delta \leq 0.060^\circ$,
${\approx}7$\,km).  To confirm that this choice does not manufacture the
signal, we swept the null-rate parameter from 1\% to 10\% in 0.5\% steps,
recomputing the threshold as $\delta_{\max} = P_0 \times 1.5^\circ$
and re-running the binomial one-sided test against each of eight subsets:
full corpus, religion-founding sites, dome/tholos/stupa (UNESCO),
dome\,+\,mound, founding sites (any category), UNESCO stupa keyword,
Wikidata Q180987 stupa corpus, and the Java-region stupa sub-corpus.
All six tier levels (A++, A+, A, C, C-, C--) are evaluated at the
primary null rates (A++/C-- $= 2\%$; A+/C- $= 4\%$; A/C $= 8\%$).

Across the full-corpus sweep, odds ratios are consistently positive
(OR\,$= 1.09$--$1.43$) and binomial $p < 0.05$ for all null rates
$\geq 3.5\%$.  Sub-corpus results are consistent: the dome/tholos/stupa
subset shows OR\,$= 2.2$--$4.0$ with $p < 0.05$ across the entire 1\%--10\%
range; founding sites OR\,$= 1.4$--$2.4$; religion-founding sites
OR\,$= 1.3$--$1.7$.  C-tier (anti-harmonic) odds ratios are uniformly
close to 1.0 and non-significant, confirming that the enrichment is
specific to harmonic proximity and not an artifact of the binomial test
or bin-width choice.

Full results are in \texttt{supplementary/audit/tier\_sensitivity\_audit.txt}
(with machine-readable CSVs \texttt{tier\_sensitivity\_part1.csv} and
\texttt{tier\_sensitivity\_part2.csv}); regenerate with
\texttt{python3 tools/generate\_audit\_tier\_sensitivity.py} or
\texttt{bash tools/update\_all\_audits.sh}.

\subsection{Dome/Spherical Monument Filter (Tests~2, 2b, 2x, 1bx)}
\label{sec:kwdaudit_dome}

\NcircKeywords{} morphological keywords from four roots
(\textit{stupa/stupas}, \textit{tholos}, \textit{dome/domed/domes},
\textit{spherical}) are matched as whole words against each site's
name, XML description, and extended OUV text. All \NcircTotal{} matching
sites are included in Test~2 without context gating.

For Test~5x, ambiguous keywords (\textit{dome, domed, domes, spherical})
are subject to a two-gate context check: Gate~1 (\NdomeGateNeg{} negative
patterns) rejects non-architectural uses; Gate~2 (\NdomeGatePos{} positive
patterns) requires architectural confirmation. Unambiguous keywords
(\textit{stupa/stupas}, \textit{tholos}) are accepted without validation.
The seven
rejected sites are: Hiroshima Peace Memorial, Richtersveld, Gyeongju,
Vi\~{n}ales Valley, Rock Islands Southern Lagoon, Uluru-Kata Tju\d{t}a,
and Diqu\'{i}s Stone Spheres.

\hyperref[sec:resultsevolution_inline]{Test~6} adds earthen mound-stage
keywords (\textit{tumulus, tumuli, barrow, barrows, kofun} unambiguous;
\textit{mound} context-validated) to trace the hemispherical tradition
from burial mound through stupa to architectural dome. Test~6x applies
the same architectural context gates to the extended population.
Statistics for all four variants are reported in
\hyperref[sec:resultscircular]{\S\ref*{sec:resultscircular}} and
\hyperref[sec:resultsevolution_inline]{\S\ref*{sec:resultsevolution_inline}}.

\subsection{Founding-Origin Classifier (Test~5, Post-Hoc)}
\label{sec:kwdaudit_founding}

\textbf{Results.} The classifier finds \foundKwApRate\% of A+ sites
carry a founding keyword versus \foundKwBaseRate\% in the full corpus
