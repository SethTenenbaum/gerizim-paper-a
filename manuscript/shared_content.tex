\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:introduction}

This study reports a statistically significant 3\textdegree{} longitudinal
periodicity in the externally curated UNESCO Cultural and Mixed World Heritage
corpus ($N = \NclusterTotal$). All deviations are computed from Mount Gerizim
(\GerizimLon\textdegree{}E), a historically documented meridian reference
(\S\ref{sec:anchor}). Sites within $\leq\ApThreshKm{}$~km of a
3\textdegree{}-grid node are designated Tier-A+ ($P_0 = \NullRateAp{}$);
the full corpus yields \NclusterAp{} A+ sites against \NclusterExpAp{} expected,
a significant excess concentrated in domed monuments (Test~2) and in harmonics
containing a precisely aligned site ($\clusterHarmonicRatio\times$ denser; Test~3).
A global anchor sweep confirms the result is stable across the
$\sim$35\textdegree{}E corridor (\S\ref{sec:sensitivity_global}).

Tier boundaries are set from angular dispersion of an independent stupa corpus
(Wikidata Q180987, $N = \vmNStupa{}$; $\hat{\sigma} = \vmSigmaDegStupa{}\textdegree{}$),
defining the $0.25\hat{\sigma}$/$0.50\hat{\sigma}$/$1.00\hat{\sigma}$ tier widths
without reference to UNESCO data.
A historical coincidence with Babylonian \textit{b\={e}ru} sub-units is noted in
Appendix~\ref{app:beru} only.

\textbf{Methodological safeguards.}
Prior longitude-alignment studies were criticised for investigator-assembled
site lists \citep{williamson1983leylines,freeman1976,kendall1974}.
This study differs: (1)~the UNESCO corpus is externally curated and fixed;
(2)~the 3\textdegree{} period is discovered from data (\S\ref{sec:limitations_geo});
(3)~results are fully reproducible \citep{tenenbaum2026audit}.
Because all inputs are fully public, every pipeline step is exploratory;
no confirmatory inference is claimed.

\subsection{Prior Work on Spatial Periodicity and Sacred Landscapes}

Statistical evaluation of spatial periodicity in monument distributions has a
long and contested history.
Thom's ``megalithic yard'' \citep{thom1967,thom1971,thom1964}
stimulated statistical refinement \citep{kendall1974} and methodological
critique \citep{freeman1976}.
Magli \citep{magli2013,magli2016} has applied Monte Carlo methods to
archaeoastronomical alignments, and Ruggles and Silva have advanced
probability-based frameworks for evaluating orientation claims
\citep{ruggles1999astronomy,ruggles2015handbook,silva2020statistical}.
The ICOMOS-IAU thematic study \citep{ruggles2010icomos} provides
institutional context for archaeoastronomical analysis of World Heritage
properties.

\subsection{The Anchor: Mount Gerizim}
\label{sec:anchor}

All harmonic deviations in this study are computed from Mount Gerizim
(\GerizimLon\textdegree{}E). Gerizim is chosen because of its independently
documented \textit{tabbur ha-aretz} (``navel of the earth'') cosmographic role
in Samaritan tradition \citep{purvis1968,macdonald1964,crown1989}; at 881~m in
the central highlands it would have served as a natural survey reference. This
justification predates the analysis entirely.

A global anchor sweep (\S\ref{sec:sensitivity_global}) confirms the result is
not sensitive to the exact coordinate: every longitude in the
\xbandLabel\textdegree{}E band produces the same enrichment, and both Gerizim
and Jerusalem (\JerusalemLon\textdegree{}E, $\approx$\GerizimJerusalemKm~km east)
place the corridor in the global top \anchorSweepTopPct\% of \anchorSweepNanchors{}
trial anchors. Mecca ($p \approx \pMeccaAnchor$, \pMeccaAnchorSig{}),
Mt.\ Meru ($p \approx \pMeruAnchor$, \pMeruAnchorSig{}), and
Mt.\ Kailash ($p \approx \pKailashAnchor$, \pKailashAnchorSig{}) are not significant.

Gerizim's coordinate is from the UNESCO tentative list (ref.~5706, DMS
\GerizimLonDMS{}, State of Palestine, 2012;
\url{https://whc.unesco.org/en/tentativelists/5706/}) and is not inscribed.
Jerusalem is an inscribed corpus member, self-excluded when used as anchor.

\subsection{Corpus Construction}
\label{sec:corpus}

The UNESCO World Heritage XML dataset \citep{unescowhc2024xml} contains
$\NwhcTotal$ inscribed nominations. UNESCO designates each site
\textit{Cultural} (criteria i--vi), \textit{Natural} (criteria vii--x),
or \textit{Mixed}; sites with cultural heritage alongside natural values
receive Mixed and are included here.
Retaining Cultural and Mixed ($-\NexcNatural$ Natural-only) and
excluding $\NexcNoCoord$ entries lacking geocoordinates (both linear
route networks) yields $N = \NclusterTotal$ sites.

\subsection{Geodetic Framework}
\label{sec:geodetic}

The reference anchor is Mount Gerizim at \GerizimLon\textdegree{}E
(see \S\ref{sec:anchor}). Gerizim is not inscribed and does not appear
in the standard corpus. Jerusalem is inscribed and scored as a regular
site in Tests~1--4 (A+ at 4.1~km under Gerizim).
For sensitivity sweeps (\S\ref{sec:sensitivity_global}), Gerizim is added
as a synthetic entry so each anchor self-excludes symmetrically.

For a site at longitude $\lambda$, the deviation from the nearest 3\textdegree{} harmonic is
$\delta = |\lambda - \GerizimLon| \bmod 3\textdegree{}$ (folded to $[0, 1.5\textdegree{}]$).
The tier classification is a fixed angular criterion.
Kilometer labels (e.g.\ $\lesssim\ApThreshKm{}$~km for Tier-A+) are equatorial
approximations computed as $\delta \times 111$~km/\textdegree{},
becoming smaller at higher latitudes; the angular criterion is consequently
conservative at high latitudes. No latitude correction is applied.

The geometric null for Tier-A+ is $2 \times \ApThreshDeg{}\textdegree{} / 1.5\textdegree{} = \NullRateApPct{}\%$,
assuming uniform longitudes. This serves as a convenient reference, but
the primary tests are simulation-based null models (\S\ref{sec:simulation_null})
that preserve the empirical longitude distribution. A Gaussian KDE produces
$\simKDEMean \pm \simKDEStd$ expected A+ (${\sim}\NullRateApPct{}\%$), confirming the
geometric null is not materially inflated by longitude clumping.


\subsection{Tier Classification}
\label{sec:tier_classification}

Tier boundaries are derived from the cross-corpus von~Mises dispersion scale
$\hat{\sigma} = \vmSigmaDegStupa{}\textdegree{}$ (stupa corpus, $\hat{\kappa} = \vmKappaStupa{}$;
see ``Von Mises cross-corpus characterisation'' below).
The three A-tiers correspond to $0.25\hat{\sigma}$, $0.50\hat{\sigma}$, and
$1.00\hat{\sigma}$ respectively.
A historical connection to Babylonian metrological sub-units is noted in
Appendix~\ref{app:beru} and \S\ref{sec:beru_coincidence}.

\begin{description}
  \item[\textbf{Tier~A++}] $0.25\hat{\sigma}$;\quad
    $\delta \leq \AppThreshDeg{}\textdegree{}$ ($\lesssim\AppThreshKm{}$~km); $P_0 = \NullRateApp{}$.
  \item[\textbf{Tier~A+}] $0.50\hat{\sigma}$ (primary threshold);\quad
    $\delta \leq \ApThreshDeg{}\textdegree{}$ ($\lesssim\ApThreshKm{}$~km); $P_0 = \NullRateAp{}$.
  \item[\textbf{Tier~A}] $1.00\hat{\sigma}$;\quad
    $\delta \leq \ATierThreshDeg{}\textdegree{}$ ($\lesssim\ATierThreshKm{}$~km); $P_0 = \NullRateA{}$.
  \item[\textbf{Tier~B}] Middle zone, not near any harmonic or inter-harmonic midpoint.
  \item[\textbf{Tier~C/C$-$/C$--$}] Symmetric mirror of A/A+/A++ measured from the
    nearest inter-harmonic midpoint.
\end{description}

\textbf{Von Mises cross-corpus characterisation.}
We fitted $f(\theta \mid \kappa) \propto e^{\kappa\cos\theta}$ (von~Mises + uniform
mixture) to the harmonic phase deviations of two external corpora:
OWTRAD Silk Road nodes ($N = \vmNOwtrad{}$; $\hat{\kappa} = \vmKappaOwtrad{}$) and
Wikidata Q180987 stupas ($N = \vmNStupa{}$; $\hat{\kappa} = \vmKappaStupa{}$).
The stupa corpus yields $\hat{\sigma} = \kappa^{-1/2} = \vmSigmaDegStupa{}\textdegree{}$;
$0.25\hat{\sigma} = \vmHalfSigmaDegStupa{}\textdegree{}$ (A++),
$0.50\hat{\sigma} = \vmOneSigmaDegStupa{}\textdegree{}$ (A+),
and $1.00\hat{\sigma} = \vmTwoSigmaDegStupa{}\textdegree{}$ (A)
match the tier thresholds to within rounding (Table~\ref{tab:vonmises_tiers}).
The $N$-weighted pooled $\hat{\sigma}$ is $\vmSigmaDegPooled{}\textdegree{}$.

\subsection{Test Populations}
\label{sec:test_populations}

Keyword populations are approximate. The raw sweep tolerates known
false positives while the context-validated variant (Test~2x) applies
sentence-level gating for greater precision.
Both populations are reported.

\textbf{Test~1 (full corpus, primary).}
Full corpus ($N = \NclusterTotal$), no keyword filter; binomial test of
whether the global A+ rate exceeds the \NullRateApPct{}\% geometric null.

\textbf{Test~2 (dome/spherical, primary).}
\NcircKeywords{} morphological keywords from four roots
(\textit{stupa/stupas}, \textit{tholos}, \textit{dome/domed/domes},
\textit{spherical}) are matched as whole words against each site's
name, XML description, and extended OUV text ($N = \NcircTotal$).
This raw sweep is over-inclusive: it admits sites where the keyword
refers to geological or vernacular features rather than monumental
architecture. The context-validated population ($N = \NcircValidated$)
is reported alongside all primary results; it yields stronger
significance, consistent with the rejected sites being
non-architectural false positives.

\textbf{Test~2b (mound evolution, sensitivity variant).}
Extends Test~2 by adding earthen mound-stage keywords
(\textit{tumulus, tumuli, barrow, barrows, kofun}).
Not counted separately in the Bonferroni family.

\textbf{Test~3 (cluster asymmetry, primary).}
Full corpus ($N = \NclusterTotal$), no keyword filter; tests whether
A+ harmonics host more total sites than non-A+ harmonics, independent
of any morphological keyword.

\textbf{Test~4 (temporal gradient, descriptive).}
Cochran-Armitage trend test on the full corpus based on year of
inscription into the UNESCO corpus, no keyword filter.

\textbf{Test~5 (founding classifier, post-hoc).}
A post-hoc keyword classifier identifies site types beyond domed monuments that fall on harmonic nodes; it carries no weight in the primary evidential chain (Appendix~\ref{sec:kwdaudit_founding}).

\subsection{Multiple-Comparisons Protocol}
\label{sec:bonferroni}

\textbf{Primary test family ($k = \BonfK$).}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Test 1} (full corpus, $N = \NclusterTotal$):
    global enrichment above \NullRateAp{} null.
  \item \textbf{Test 2} (domed/spherical monuments, $N = \NcircTotal$):
    morphological sub-population enriched above \NullRateAp{} null.
  \item \textbf{Test 3} (cluster asymmetry, full corpus, no keyword filter):
    A+-bearing harmonics host more total sites.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Other tests.}
Test~4 (temporal gradient, Cochran-Armitage) is descriptive context;
a Mantel-Haenszel stratified test and logistic regression with region as a
covariate assess the gradient robustly (\S\ref{sec:resultstemporal});
it is not part of the primary family.
Test~2b is a sensitivity variant of Test~2.
Test~5 (founding classifier) is post-hoc (Appendix~\ref{sec:kwdaudit_founding}).
World religion sub-group analyses (\S\ref{sec:resultsrelig}) use Fisher exact tests and are not Bonferroni-corrected.
Tests~A--D (\S\ref{sec:results_rayleigh}) assess the 3\textdegree{} periodicity
structure: Test~A (full-corpus Rayleigh) is a negative control structurally
insensitive to the sparse A+ sub-population; Tests~C and~D are positive tests;
Test~B is tautological by construction and excluded from both tables.

\section{Results}

\label{sec:results}

\subsection{Phase-Invariant Evidence for the 3\textdegree{} Period}
\label{sec:results_phase_invariant}

Three results establish the 3\textdegree{} periodicity \emph{without
reference to any anchor}, addressing the concern that anchor-based binning
could fabricate apparent structure.
(i)~The A+ Rayleigh phase-lock test on the 3\textdegree{}-circle yields
$p_\mathrm{perm} = \rayleighPermP$~\rayleighPermPSig{}
(\S\ref{sec:phase_peak_formal}).
(ii)~A phase-envelope scan over all origins
$\phi \in [0,3\textdegree{})$ (\phaseNphases{} phases at \phaseStep\textdegree{}
resolution) shows a strong preferred phase: full-corpus A+ counts span
\phaseFullMin{}--\phaseFullMax{} (median \phaseFullMedian{}), and Gerizim's phase
($\phaseGerizimPhase\textdegree{}$ mod 3\textdegree{}) sits at the
\phaseFullGerPctile\textsuperscript{th} percentile (\phaseFullGerCount{} A+),
the \phaseDomeGerPctile\textsuperscript{th} for domes
(\phaseDomeGerCount/\NcircTotal{}; range \phaseDomeMin{}--\phaseDomeMax{}).
(iii)~A degree-denominated Monte Carlo unit sweep identifies
3\textdegree{} as the dominant period (\S\ref{sec:limitations_geo}).
The signal is therefore phase-localised, not phase-uniform; subsequent
tests characterise it relative to Gerizim, the historically attested phase
reference (\S\ref{sec:anchor}).

\subsection{Global Enrichment (Test~1, Primary)}
\label{sec:results_global}

The full UNESCO Cultural/Mixed corpus ($N = \NclusterTotal$) shows a
Tier-A+ rate of \clusterApRate\% (Figure~\ref{fig:devhist})
(\NclusterAp/\NclusterTotal; Clopper-Pearson 95\% CI

above the \NullRateAp{} null
(binomial $p = \clusterApBinom$; Bonferroni $p_{\mathrm{adj}} = \pAdjTestOne$,
$k = \BonfK$).
The observed enrichment is $\clusterEnrichAp\times$, modest relative
to the dome subpopulation ($\circEnrichAp\times$) and the harmonic cluster
asymmetry. For the primary family (Tests~1--3, $k=\BonfK$), 
all three pass the Bonferroni bound at $k = \BonfK$
(full corpus $p_\mathrm{adj} = \pAdjTestOne$~\pAdjTestOneSig{},
dome $p_\mathrm{adj} = \pAdjTestTwo$~\pAdjTestTwoSig{},
cluster $p_\mathrm{adj} = \pAdjTestThree$~\pAdjTestThreeSig{}).

\textbf{Region-stratified robustness.}
A region-specific empirical null (Fisher combination of per-region binomials)
yields $p = \pRegionEmpAll$ (\pRegionEmpAllSig{}); the pre-2000 cohort remains significant
against the geometric null ($p = \pPreTwoK$~\pPreTwoKSig{}; \S\ref{sec:resultstemporal}).
Both results are included in the FDR audit (Table~\ref{tab:bonferroni_both}).

\subsubsection*{World religion sites}
\label{sec:resultsrelig}
Religion sub-populations are defined by keyword sets documented in the supplementary audit archive~\citep{tenenbaum2026audit}.
A chi-squared test finds no significant A+ rate difference across the five main
religion groups ($\chi^2 = \religChiSqStat$, df\,=\,\religChiSqDof,
$p = \pReligChiSq$, \pReligChiSqSig{}).
Both Buddhism (\NbudATier/\NbudSitesRelig{} A-tier, \budATierRate\%;
\NbudInterHarmonic/\NbudSitesRelig{} C-band, \budInterHarmonicRate\%;
$p = \pBudJoint$~\pBudJointSig{}) and Hinduism (\NhinATier/\NhinSites{} A-tier, \hinATierRate\%;
\NhinInterHarmonic/\NhinSites{} C-band, \hinInterHarmonicRate\%;
$p = \pHinJoint$~\pHinJointSig{}) show a joint A-tier/C-band signal
(multivariate hypergeometric); both persist in tradition-exclusive sites
($p = \pBudExclJoint$~\pBudExclJointSig{}, $p = \pHinExclJoint$~\pHinExclJointSig{}).

\subsection{Domed and Spherical Monuments (Test~2, Primary)}
\label{sec:resultscircular}

The keyword filter is described in \S\ref{sec:test_populations} ($N = \NcircTotal$).

\textbf{Tier-A++ concentration (key result).}
\NcircTierApp{} of \NcircTotal{} dome sites (\NcircAppRate\%, $\circEnrichApp\times$ the \NullRateApp{} null)
fall within Tier-A++ ($\delta \leq \AppThreshDeg{}\textdegree{}$, $\lesssim\AppThreshKm{}$~km).
This concentration is significant and reproducible across three test statistics:
Fisher OR~$= \circAppFisherOR$, $p = \pCircAppFisher$~\pCircAppFisherSig{};
permutation $Z = \circAppPermZ$, $p = \circAppPermP$~\pCircAppPermSig{};
bootstrap $p = \circAppBootP$~\pCircAppBootSig{}.
Dome A++ survives full Bonferroni correction at $k = \NtotalTests$ (\S\ref{sec:multiple_comparisons}).

\textbf{Tier-A+ enrichment (Bonferroni primary).}
\NcircTierAp/\NcircTotal{} sites at Tier-A+
(\NcircApRate\% [\circApCIlo, \circApCIhi], $\circEnrichAp\times$;
Fisher OR~$= \circApFisherOR$, $p = \pCircApFisher$~\pCircApFisherSig{};
binomial $p = \pCircAp$~\pCircApSig{};
Bonferroni $p_\mathrm{adj} = \pAdjTestTwo$~\pAdjTestTwoSig{}, $k = \BonfK$).
Tier-A: \NcircTierA/\NcircTotal{} (\circARate\%, $\circEnrichA\times$;
Fisher $p = \pCircAFisher$~\pCircAFisherSig{};
binomial $p = \pCircA$~\pCircASig{}).
The $\chi^2$-uniform test ($p = \pCircChi$, \pCircChiSig{}) is consistent with
concentration at harmonic nodes.
Tier-B (middle zone): \NcircTierB/\NcircTotal{} sites;
mean $\delta = \circMeanDev$\textdegree{}.

\noindent\textbf{Tier-A+ sites}:
Khoja Ahmed Yasawi (0.2~km),
Boyana Church (0.3~km),
Lumbini (0.8~km),
Humayun's Tomb (2.0~km),
Pyu Ancient Cities (2.3~km),
Silk Roads: Chang'an-Tianshan (2.5~km), Trulli of Alberobello (3.6~km),
Old City of Jerusalem (4.1~km),
Borobudur (4.3~km),

A leave-one-out binomial check confirms no single site drives the result:
removing any A+ site yields $N = \LOOdomeN$, A+$= \LOOdomeAp$,
$p = \LOOdomeP$.
Leave-2-out (worst case) across all $\binom{\LKOnAp}{2}$ combinations:
$p = \LKOworstTwoP$.
Leave-3-out (worst case): $p = \LKOworstThreeP$; the evolution corpus (Test~2b) (\S\ref{sec:resultsevolution_inline}) 
and the A++ concentration are more robust. 
The A++ leave-3-out worst case across all
$\binom{\LKOAppNApp}{3} = \LKOAppNCombosThreeApp$ triples yields
$p = \LKOAppworstThreeP$, remaining significant at $\alpha = 0.05$
(leave-2-out worst case $p = \LKOAppworstTwoP$).

\textbf{Context-validated robustness (Tests~2x).}
Sentence-level confirmation reduces the dome corpus to $N = \NcircValidated$
(\NcircContextRejected{} false positives rejected; Appendix~\ref{sec:kwdaudit_dome}).
Test~2x Fisher exact (dome sub-pop vs.\ full corpus, $N = \NclusterTotal$):
A++ (\NcircValidatedApp/\NcircValidated, $\circAppValidatedFisherOR\times$,
$p = \pCircAppValidatedFisher$~\pCircAppValidatedFisherSig{});
A+ (\NcircValidatedAp/\NcircValidated, $\circApValidatedFisherOR\times$,
$p = \pCircApValidatedFisher$~\pCircApValidatedFisherSig{});
A (\NcircValidatedA/\NcircValidated, $\circAValidatedFisherOR\times$,
$p = \pCircAValidatedFisher$~\pCircAValidatedFisherSig{}).

\textbf{Keyword-stratified sensitivity (leave-stupa-out).}
Partitioning the dome population by triggering keyword shows that stupa/stupas
($n = \kwSensStupaN$) has the strongest individual signal
(A++ OR~$= \kwSensStupaAppOR$, $p = \kwSensStupaAppP$~\kwSensStupaAppPSig{}),
but dome/tholos/spherical alone ($n = \kwSensNoStupaN$, no stupa keyword)
remains independently significant at A++
(OR~$= \kwSensNoStupaAppOR$, $p = \kwSensNoStupaAppP$~\kwSensNoStupaAppPSig{}).
Mound keywords (tumulus/barrow/kofun) show no enrichment ($n = \kwSensMoundN$),
confirming the filter is morphologically discriminating.

\textbf{Geographic-concentration null (dedicated simulation).}
\label{sec:geo_conc_null}
Domed monuments are concentrated in the Eurasian corridor:
\domeEurasianFraction\% fall in 20\textdegree--120\textdegree{}E
versus \fullCorpusEurasianFraction\% of the full corpus.
Two targeted null models test whether this explains the dome enrichment
(\simNperms{} trials).

\textbf{Null~A (within-dome bootstrap):} Draw $N = \NcircTotal$ longitudes
from the dome sites' own list. Mean $= \geoNullDomeBootMean$ A+,
$p = \geoNullDomeBootP$ (\geoNullDomeBootPSig{}). The dome sites' positions are concentrated
near harmonic longitudes.

\textbf{Null~C (restricted geographic draw):} Pool all full-corpus sites
within $\pm W$\textdegree{} of any dome site and bootstrap the expected A+
count. Dome sites (\NcircTierAp~A+, \NcircApRate\%) significantly exceed
the broader footprint at all three windows tested:
$\pm 2$\textdegree{} ($p = \geoNullDomeRestrictedPtwo$, \geoNullDomeRestrictedPtwoSig{});
$\pm 5$\textdegree{} ($p = \geoNullDomeRestrictedP$, \geoNullDomeRestrictedPSig{});
$\pm 10$\textdegree{} ($p = \geoNullDomeRestrictedPten$, \geoNullDomeRestrictedPtenSig{}).
Results are window-independent.

\textbf{Reconciliation.}
Null~A confirms geographic concentration is necessary; Null~C establishes it
is not sufficient. Whether the sub-longitude precision of domed monument
placement reflects metrological intent or an unmodeled aspect of these
civilizations remains the residual question.

\paragraph*{Methodological note: Rayleigh phase-lock (Test~B).}
\label{sec:results_rayleigh}
The near-perfect Rayleigh $R$ of the A+ sub-sample is a mathematical
consequence of the A+ classification criterion (\S\ref{sec:circularity})
and is reported as a structural description.
Three formal permutation tests (\NpermRayleigh{} shuffles each, seed~\simSeed{})
address distinct aspects of the
periodicity:

\textbf{Test~A (full-corpus Rayleigh, negative control).}
The full-corpus Rayleigh $R = \fullRayleighR$
($Z = \fullRayleighZ$, $p_\mathrm{perm} = \fullRayleighPermP$, \fullRayleighPermPSig{})
confirms no spurious global 3\textdegree{} periodicity is present in the
raw longitude file. The signal is concentrated in A+ sites, not a
global artifact.

\textbf{Test~B (A+-only Rayleigh, structural consistency check).}
Among the \Nap{} Tier-A+ sites, $R = \rayleighR$ (\rayleighRPctPhrasing{} sites
share the same phase modulo 3\textdegree{}; $Z = \rayleighZ$).
This is a structural consequence of the A+ classification criterion, not
independent evidence; it is reported for completeness only
(\S\ref{sec:circularity}).

\textbf{Test~C (circular-shift anchor sensitivity).}
Displacing the anchor by a uniform random offset in $[0^\circ,
360^\circ)$ and recounting A+ sites yields a null mean of
\anchorShiftNullMu{} vs.\ the observed \anchorShiftApCount{}
($Z = \anchorShiftZ$, $p_\mathrm{perm} = \anchorShiftPermP$, \anchorShiftPermPSig{}).
A positive anchor-specific test complementary to Test~D.

\textbf{Test~D (targeted von~Mises mixture, full corpus).}
A von~Mises/uniform two-component mixture is fit to all $N = \NoriginCorpus$
folded angles with $\mu_0$ fixed at the Gerizim phase; $\hat{w}$ and
$\hat{\kappa}$ estimated by EM; $D = 2(\hat{\ell} - \ell_0)$ referred to a
parametric bootstrap ($B = \targetedB$) for an empirical $p$-value.
On the full corpus: $D = \targetedDfull$,
$\hat{w} = \targetedWfull$ (i.e.\ ${\approx}\targetedWfullPct\%$ of all
sites), $\hat{\kappa} = \targetedKfull$
(angular SD $\approx \targetedSdFullDeg$\textdegree{}),
$p_\mathrm{boot} = \targetedPfull$ (\targetedPfullSig{}).
On the A+ subset alone: $D = \targetedDAp$, $\hat{w} = \targetedWAp$,
$\hat{\kappa} = \targetedKAp$ (angular SD $\approx \targetedSdApDeg$\textdegree{}),
$p_\mathrm{boot} = \targetedPAp$ (\targetedPApSig{}).
Tests~B--D together confirm that the 3\textdegree{} periodicity is anchor-specific and not a statistical artifact.

\textbf{Test~E (anchor discovery correction).}
A global sweep of \anchorSweepNanchors{} trial anchors asks whether the
Levantine corridor ranks unusually among all possible anchors
($p = \anchorMaxPermP$, \anchorMaxPermPSig{}).
This is a conservative upper bound on the anchor-selection penalty;
primary evidence rests on Tests~2 and~3, which do not depend on the anchor choice
(\S\ref{sec:limitations_circularity}).

\section{Robustness Checks}
\label{sec:robustness}

\subsection{Unit Sensitivity Sweep}
\label{sec:metrology}

Varying harmonic spacing from 1.5\textdegree{} to 6\textdegree{} (physical threshold
fixed at \ApThreshKm{}~km), the 3\textdegree{} spacing produces the strongest joint
significance across both the dome and full-corpus populations.
The 1.5\textdegree{} and 6\textdegree{} spacings also reach significance, but both are
mathematical consequences of the 3\textdegree{} structure: every 3\textdegree{}
harmonic is also a 1.5\textdegree{} harmonic, and 6\textdegree{} harmonics are a strict
subset, so neither constitutes an independent signal.
Adjacent non-harmonic spacings (1.8\textdegree{}, 2.1\textdegree{}, 2.7\textdegree{}, 3.3\textdegree{}, 3.6\textdegree{}) are
non-significant in either population, the key discriminating observation.
The 2.4\textdegree{} spacing reaches nominal dome significance
($p = \sweepEightDomeP$) but not full-corpus ($p = \sweepEightFullP$, \sweepEightFullPSig{}),
reflecting unsystematic local overlap with the 3\textdegree{} grid rather than
a genuine secondary periodicity.
Specifically, \sweepEightTenDomeBoth{}/\sweepZeroEightDomeHits{} 
(\sweepEightTenDomePctOfEight\%) of 2.4\textdegree{} dome hits align 
to the 3\textdegree{} grid, indicating the significance is due to overlap.

A degree-denominated Monte Carlo sweep
(\S\ref{sec:limitations_geo}) confirms this pattern. Candidate intervals
at 4\textdegree{} and 8\textdegree{} reach nominal dome significance, but
inspection shows that 6 of 7 and all 4 of their hits respectively fall on
12\textdegree{} and 24\textdegree{} nodes shared with the 3\textdegree{}
grid, confirming they are consequences of the primary periodicity rather
than independent angular units.

\subsection{Sensitivity Slope at the Canonical Unit}
\label{sec:sensitivity_slope_perm}

A fine sweep within $\pm 1\%$ of 3\textdegree{}
shows a $\pm 0.3\%$ shift collapses joint significance in at least one
population. A geographic bootstrap of the full cultural corpus finds no
permutation in \permSlopeSpecN{} trials that reproduces the canonical-unit
sharp peak at either the A$^{+}$ or A$^{++}$ precision tier
($p = \permSlopeCanonBestJointFullAP{}$ and
$p = \permSlopeCanonBestJointFullAPP{}$ respectively,
both \permSlopeCanonBestJointSigFullAP{}), confirming 3\textdegree{} as a specific
periodicity. The dome-filter specificity test yields marginal results across all
tiers (A$^{++}$: $p = \permSlopeCanonBestJointDomeAPP{}$,
\permSlopeCanonBestJointSigDomeAPP{}; A$^{+}$: $p = \permSlopeCanonBestJointDomeAP{}$,
\permSlopeCanonBestJointSigDomeAP{}; A: $p = \permSlopeCanonBestJointDomeA{}$,
\permSlopeCanonBestJointSigDomeA{}), consistent with the test being underpowered
at $n = \NcircValidated{}$ sites. Restricting to the \permSlopeKwN{}
morphologically unambiguous spherical forms (stupa, tholos) recovers
significance at the A tier
($p = \permSlopeCanonBestJointKwA{}$, \permSlopeCanonBestJointSigKwA{}). The 3\textdegree{} periodicity is
independently confirmed by Tests~C and~D (\S\ref{sec:results_rayleigh}).

A log-scale threshold sweep (\logSweepNtaus{} values,
\logSweepAregionNsig/\logSweepAregionNtaus{} significant within $\pm$1
log-decade of A+) yields dome-population data-optimal bands
A$^{++}{\approx}\logSweepOptPtauKm{}$~km,
A$^{+}{\approx}\logSweepOptApKm{}$~km,
A${\approx}\logSweepOptAKm{}$~km
(geometric ratio $r^{*}{=}\logSweepOptRatio{}$);
the analytically derived tier boundaries (\AppThreshDeg{}\textdegree{}, \ApThreshDeg{}\textdegree{}, \ATierThreshDeg{}\textdegree{})
are the nearest $\hat{\sigma}$-multiple boundaries to these optima.

(90\textdegree--105\textdegree{}E), is A-tier depleted (combined OR $= \wikiHeartlandATierOR\times$,

but strongly enriched in the inter-harmonic C-band:

\section{Limitations}
\label{sec:limitations}

\subsection{Circularity: Full Canonical Treatment}
\label{sec:limitations_circularity}
\label{sec:circularity}

Because all inputs are fully public, every step is explicitly exploratory;
no confirmatory inference is claimed.
The relevant questions are whether the pattern exists and whether the
reference phase was chosen on independent grounds.

Both are answered directly. The global anchor sweep (\S\ref{sec:sensitivity_global})
confirms that the enrichment is a property of the $\sim$35\textdegree{}E
corridor: every $\xbandLabel$\textdegree{}E anchor produces the same result, and
Gerizim and Jerusalem both place the corridor in the global top
\anchorSweepTopPct\% of \anchorSweepNanchors{} trial anchors.
Gerizim is distinguished by pre-existing documentary evidence—the \textit{tabbur ha-aretz}
cosmographic role (\S\ref{sec:anchor})—predating this analysis.

The 3\textdegree{} structure is recoverable by any unanchored
\NharmonicsCoarse-phase scan. Dome enrichment (Test~2) is identical across
all \NharmonicsCoarse{} $\xbandLabel$\textdegree{}E anchors. Cluster
asymmetry (Test~3) requires no keyword filter and no anchor.
Anchor-discovery correction (Test~E, $p = \anchorMaxPermP$,
\anchorMaxPermPSig{}) is marginal; primary evidence is Tests~2 and~3.

\subsection{Geographic and Cultural Selection Bias}
\label{sec:limitations_geo}

The UNESCO list over-represents Europe and the
Mediterranean-to-South-Asian corridor of Cultural/Mixed sites. 
The regional distribution is as follows: 
(Europe \& North America: \CorpusPctEurope\%;
Arab States: \CorpusPctArabStates\%;
Asia-Pacific: \CorpusPctAsiaPac\%;
Latin Americas: \CorpusPctLatAm\%;
Africa: \CorpusPctAfrica\%).
Domed monuments exceed the A+ rate of other monuments from the same longitude
footprint (Null~C, $p = \geoNullDomeRestrictedP$, \geoNullDomeRestrictedPSig{}), establishing
that the enrichment is carried by architectural type, not location alone.

A Monte Carlo unit sweep (\mcNperms{} permutations, \mcNcandidates{} candidate
spacing values from 0.5\textdegree{} to 15\textdegree{}) identifies which
angular interval produces clustering beyond geographic concentration without
assuming any particular unit. The dominant peak is at 3\textdegree{} ($p < 0.001$),
with harmonics at 6\textdegree{} and 12\textdegree{} also significant.
Collapsing harmonics, the dome subset yields \mcDomeNsigCollapsed{} independent
dominant spacings ($P = \mcDomeCollapsedPEqOne{}$ under the geographic null),
supporting 3\textdegree{} as the period to which domed monuments are preferentially aligned.

\subsection{Americas Negative Control}
\label{sec:americas_negative_control}
The UNESCO Americas sub-corpus ($N = \AmericasN$) shows an A+ rate of
\AmericasApRate\%, below the \NullRateAp{} null
(one-sided depletion: $p = \AmericasOneSidedP$~\AmericasOneSidedStar{}).
The sample is too small to treat this as strong confirmatory depletion,
but it does not contradict the primary result.

\subsection{Other Limitations}

\textbf{Geocoding precision.}
UNESCO XML coordinates are administrative centroids: unsystematic noise,
no directional bias.

\textbf{Metrological evidence.}
No surveying instrument calibrated in ancient metrological sub-units has
been recovered; the bēru system
\citep{hunger2019mul,robson2008mathematics,powell1987metrological} is noted
as historical coincidence only. All thresholds are degree-denominated
(cross-corpus angular dispersion).

\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}

\subsection{Alternative Explanations}
\label{sec:alt_explanations}

\textbf{(1) Independent convergence.}
Hemispherical structures recur across cultures
\citep{aveni2001skywatchers,aveni2003archaeoastronomy}, but convergence
predicts no longitude specificity.

\textbf{(2) Geographic concentration.}
Null~C: other monuments from the same longitude footprint are
significantly less enriched ($p = \geoNullDomeRestrictedP$,
\geoNullDomeRestrictedPSig{}); the Monte Carlo unit sweep
(\S\ref{sec:limitations_geo}) independently confirms 3\textdegree{} without
assuming any unit.

\textbf{(3) Random coincidence.}
The KDE-smoothed null confirms dome enrichment ($p = \simKDEP$~\simKDEPSig{});
permutation ($p = \simDomePermP$~\simDomePermPSig{}) and bootstrap
($p = \simDomeBootP$~\simDomeBootPSig{}) are marginal at the A+ level
but highly significant for the A++ sub-population
(permutation $p = \circAppPermP$~\pCircAppPermSig{};
bootstrap $p = \circAppBootP$~\pCircAppBootSig{}).
Tests~C and~D (\S\ref{sec:results_rayleigh}) confirm anchor specificity and periodicity structure.

\subsection{Independent Validation: OWTRAD and Wikidata Corpora}
\label{sec:transmission}

The 3\textdegree{} period identified in the UNESCO corpus is independently
confirmed in two external datasets. First, the OWTRAD attested-route
network \citep{ciolek2004owtrad} (\NOwtradEdges{} route segments,
\NOwtradVertices{} unique endpoints).
Testing all \NOwtradDegW{} endpoint positions across the full edge list
(each city weighted by the number of segments it connects),
\NOwtradDegWApp{} fall at A++ harmonics, \NOwtradDegWAp{} at A+, and \NOwtradDegWA{} at A
($\owtradDegWAppEnrich\times$ null rate, $z = \zOwtradDegWApp$,
$p = \pOwtradDegWApp$~\pOwtradDegWAppSig{} for A++;
$p = \pOwtradDegWAp$~\pOwtradDegWApSig{} for A+;
$p = \pOwtradDegWA$~\pOwtradDegWASig{} for A; one-tailed binomial).
Testing each unique endpoint once gives
\NOwtradVertexApp{}/\NOwtradVertices{} at A++, \NOwtradVertexAp{} at A+, and \NOwtradVertexA{} at A
($\owtradVertexAppEnrich\times$, $z = \zOwtradVertexApp$,
$p = \pOwtradVertexApp$~\pOwtradVertexAppSig{} for A++;
$p = \pOwtradVertexAp$~\pOwtradVertexApSig{} for A+;
$p = \pOwtradVertexA$~\pOwtradVertexASig{} for A; one-tailed binomial).
A phase-shift test (random offset of the 3\textdegree{} grid,
\NOwtradPerms{} iterations) gives $p = \pOwtradVertexAppPhase$~\pOwtradVertexAppPhaseSig{},
confirming the enrichment is specific to the Gerizim anchor.
A cluster asymmetry test mirrors Test~3 of the primary family:
harmonics containing at least one A++ endpoint (\NOwtradAppHarmonics{} harmonics)
carry \owtradClustDegRatio$\times$ more total route-connectivity than
harmonics without one (\NOwtradNonAppHarmonics{} harmonics;
$p = \pOwtradClustDeg$~\pOwtradClustDegSig{}, Mann-Whitney one-tailed).
The Silk Road corridor is illustrated in Figure~\ref{fig:geo_trail}; harmonic tier distributions for both OWTRAD populations are shown in Figure~\ref{fig:owtrad_tiers}.

\section{Conclusion}

The UNESCO Cultural and Mixed corpus shows significant 3\textdegree{} longitudinal
enrichment with all three primary tests surviving Bonferroni correction at $k=\BonfK$.
Longitude bands containing a precisely aligned site are $\clusterHarmonicRatio\times$ more
densely populated than bands without one. High-precision alignment concentrates in earlier
inscription cohorts. The period is recoverable without specifying an anchor in advance,
indicating a phase-repeating structure whose maximum coincides with the Levantine corridor;
Mount Gerizim is a historically documented point within that corridor.
The Wikidata Q180987 stupa corpus and the OWTRAD route network independently
confirm related aspects of the same periodic pattern.

\section{The B\={e}ru Unit and Metrological Background}
\label{app:beru}

The \textit{b\={e}ru} (30\textdegree{} of arc) is attested in the MUL.APIN
cuneiform compendium \citep{hunger2019mul,watson2011writing}, dated to the late
second or early first millennium~BCE.
The unit divided the ecliptic into twelve segments and served for both stellar
and terrestrial angular measurement (MUL.APIN~II~ii~43--iii~15).
Sub-division into 30~\textit{u\v{s}} (1~u\v{s}~$= 1$\textdegree{}) is well
attested \citep{hunger2019mul,robson2008mathematics}.
The b\={e}ru also functioned as a unit of territorial description: royal
inscriptions record inter-regional distances in b\={e}ru, and
\textit{kudurru} boundary stones deposit territorial extents as legally
authoritative records \citep{robson2008mathematics,powell1987metrological}.
Meridian transit observation and eclipse timing are sufficient to derive
inter-site angular separations at 1\textdegree{} resolution
\citep{hunger2019mul,neugebauer1975history}.

\textbf{Why 0.1 b\={e}ru?}
The 3\textdegree{} (0.1-b\={e}ru) grid spacing yields \NharmonicsCoarse{}
equally spaced harmonic nodes covering the full 360\textdegree{} longitudinal
domain. Coarser spacings are uninformative and finer ones produce no signal
(\S\ref{sec:metrology}).
The choice of a base-10 fraction of the attested b\={e}ru is motivated by
uniform divisibility, not by evidence for a discrete 3\textdegree{} unit in
the cuneiform record \citep{ruggles1999astronomy,silva2020statistical}.

\textbf{Metrological coincidence with tier boundaries.}
Thureau-Dangin~\citep[p.~133]{thureau1921} derives the linear
distance-b\={e}ru from 180~\textit{aslu} of 59.4\,m, giving
${\approx}10{,}692$\,m; at 111\,km/\textdegree{} this is
${\approx}0.00321$~angular-b\={e}ru.
Two parasangs equal one distance-b\={e}ru
\citep[p.~467]{powell1987metrological} (${\approx}0.00160$~angular-b\={e}ru),
and one barid equals two distance-b\={e}rus
\citep[vol.~2, p.~63, n.~2]{burton1893pilgrimage}
(${\approx}0.00642$~angular-b\={e}ru).
These values are numerically equal (to rounding) to the
$0.25\hat{\sigma}$/$0.50\hat{\sigma}$/$1.00\hat{\sigma}$ tier boundaries
derived from the von~Mises fit (\S\ref{sec:tier_classification}), and are
presented as a historical coincidence, not as the primary derivation.

\subsection{Dome/Spherical Monument Filter (Tests~2, 2b, 2x, 2bx)}
\label{sec:kwdaudit_dome}

\NcircKeywords{} morphological keywords from four roots
(\textit{stupa/stupas}, \textit{tholos}, \textit{dome/domed/domes},
\textit{spherical}) are matched as whole words against each site's
name, XML description, and extended OUV text. All \NcircTotal{} matching
sites are included in Test~2 without context gating.

For Test~2x, ambiguous keywords (\textit{dome, domed, domes, spherical})
are subject to a two-gate context check: Gate~1 (\NdomeGateNeg{} negative
patterns) rejects non-architectural uses; Gate~2 (\NdomeGatePos{} positive
patterns) requires architectural confirmation. Unambiguous keywords
(\textit{stupa/stupas}, \textit{tholos}) are accepted without validation.
The seven
rejected sites are: Hiroshima Peace Memorial, Richtersveld, Gyeongju,
Vi\~{n}ales Valley, Rock Islands Southern Lagoon, Uluru-Kata Tju\d{t}a,
and Diqu\'{i}s Stone Spheres.

\hyperref[sec:resultsevolution_inline]{Test~2b} adds earthen mound-stage
keywords (\textit{tumulus, tumuli, barrow, barrows, kofun} unambiguous;
\textit{mound} context-validated) to trace the hemispherical tradition
from burial mound through stupa to architectural dome. Test~2bx applies
the same architectural context gates to the extended population.
Statistics for all four variants are reported in
\hyperref[sec:resultscircular]{§\ref*{sec:resultscircular}} and
\hyperref[sec:resultsevolution_inline]{§\ref*{sec:resultsevolution_inline}}.

\subsection{Founding-Origin Classifier (Test~5, Post-Hoc)}
\label{sec:kwdaudit_founding}

\textbf{Results.} The classifier finds \foundKwApRate\% of A+ sites
carry a founding keyword versus \foundKwBaseRate\% in the full corpus
(Fisher OR~$= \foundKwFisherOR$, $p = \pFoundKwFisher$~\pFoundKwFisherSig{}).
The A++ sub-population (\NfoundKwTotalApp{} sites) shows a stronger
signal: \foundKwAppRate\% carry a founding keyword
(OR~$= \foundKwAppFisherOR$, $p = \pFoundKwAppFisher$~\pFoundKwAppFisherSig{}),
consistent with the most precisely aligned sites being disproportionately
historically foundational monuments.

The classifier assigns sites to five founding-related categories; full
keyword definitions and the site-by-site listing are in the supplementary
archive \citep{tenenbaum2026audit}.
